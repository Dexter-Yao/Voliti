01 · The Problem

Fat loss fails not from ignorance,
but from moments of weakness

We interviewed ~35 real users across China and global markets, including Xiaohongshu, Reddit, and in-depth conversations. We found a universal struggle that crosses cultures and age groups:

"I know everything I need to know. I just can't control myself. I just need someone with me."
— Xiaohongshu user
"I already know this. Being consistent is the problem for me."
— Reddit r/loseit, "tried 40 times in 7 years"

Existing fat loss tools (calorie counters, fitness trackers, diet apps) all solve the "don't know how" problem. But what users actually lack is someone to help them make the right choice when they're stressed, tired, or emotionally overwhelmed.

02 · The Solution

An AI coach that knows your patterns
and shows up at the right moment

Voliti acts like a calm strategic advisor. It won't motivate you with streaks and rewards. Instead, it helps you see your own behavior patterns, prepares you for high-risk moments, and intervenes gently when you're about to drift.

01
Daily Coach Rhythm
Morning state check-in, micro-interventions at key moments during the day, evening reflection on today's choices. Three touchpoints that form a continuous behavior change process.
02
LifeSign System
Pre-set coping plans for vulnerable moments while you're clear-headed. "If I feel tired at 3pm and want snacks, then drink water and walk for 5 minutes first." Replace willpower with planning.
03
Temporal Awareness
The coach remembers your life timeline: last week's business trip, this Friday's dinner party, recurring weekend loss-of-control patterns. It predicts risk in advance, not after the fact.
04
Witness System
When you hit a milestone, the coach generates a personalized commemorative card. Not a reward, but a record: "I saw what you did." Something to look back on during low moments and find strength.

04 · Critical Moment Intervention

3 PM. The moment
fatigue hits.

3 PM is Alex's recurring vulnerable moment. Three meetings back to back, brain fried, automatically thinking about the cake at the cafe downstairs.

At this moment, the coach won't wait until he finishes the cake to say "you went over today." Instead, it gently surfaces his pre-made plan right when the craving hits.

Why this moment matters: Users lose control not because they don't know what to do, but because under fatigue/stress, the brain automatically picks the path of least resistance. The coach's job is to offer an alternative at the exact moment "autopilot" kicks in.

06 · Witness Moments

Being seen,
not being rewarded

When a user achieves a milestone, the coach generates a Witness Card, a personalized commemorative card.

This is not a "congratulations, you earned a gold medal" gamification reward. It's the coach saying: "I recorded what you did." Psychology research shows that informational feedback supports intrinsic motivation, while controlling rewards erode it.

High-value scenario: The coach discovers progress the user hasn't noticed themselves (e.g., consistent weekday breakfast structure for three weeks straight), and proactively generates a Witness Card. This experience of "being seen" is the core value of the coaching relationship.

Scene: Pattern Recognition → Plan Creation
After detecting a three-week Friday pattern, the coach doesn't say "you eat too much on Fridays." Instead: "This is a signal. Let's prepare."
Plan Lifecycle:
1. Coach detects pattern → proposes creating a plan
2. User and coach co-create the plan (user has edit rights)
3. Next time it triggers, the coach proactively reminds
4. Post-execution tracking of effectiveness
5. Ineffective plans get iteratively revised

Psychology research shows that "if-then plans" (implementation intentions) are far more effective for long-term behavior maintenance than action plans alone. The key: prepare for vulnerable moments while clear-headed, replacing on-the-spot willpower consumption with pre-made plans.

08 · User Validation

Who needs this product

We identified four core user segments. Their shared trait: they don't lack knowledge. They lack execution support at critical moments.

~40%
Execution Strugglers
"I know what to do, I just can't do it." Knowledge workers whose habits break due to travel, overtime, and social obligations. Need minimum viable consistency.
~30%
Emotional Eaters
"I know what triggers me, but I can't stop." Use food to process anxiety and boredom. Need to be understood, not judged.
~20%
Serial Dieters
"This time is different... until it isn't." Average of 5-10 diet attempts. Need someone to help them see recurring failure patterns.
~10%
Maintenance Challengers
"I did it, but I don't know if I can keep it." Already lost weight, but maintaining is harder than losing. Need to shift from dieting mindset to lifestyle mindset.

10 · Architecture

How it works

Voliti has three layers: the user faces a coach conversation on their phone, backed by a continuously running AI system.

User Layer
iOS App
The user's only interface. Two views: COACH (dialogue) and MIRROR (data reflection). Local storage ensures offline availability.
↕
Coach Layer
AI Coach Agent
The core engine. Understands conversation context, identifies behavior patterns, manages the LifeSign system, and delivers timely interventions. Built-in behavior change methodology.
Analysis System
Background process that periodically scans user timelines, identifies cross-week behavior trends and high-risk patterns, and generates summaries for the Coach.
↕
Data Layer
Behavior Log
Every meal, state check-in, and weight record stored as event files with timestamps, context, and raw evidence.
Timeline
Bidirectional timeline: retrospective behavior patterns + prospective upcoming life events. The coach's temporal awareness is built on this.
Plan Library
User's LifeSign plans with trigger conditions, coping behaviors, and success rates. Continuously refined through usage.
Key Design Principle: The user only faces one coach. All analysis, pattern recognition, and data processing happen behind the scenes. What the user experiences is a calm, steady, understanding conversation partner.
